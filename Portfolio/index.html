About Me

Hi, my name is Sabir Aliyev. I was born on August 9, 2005, in the beautiful city of Tbilisi. I completed high school in 2023 and began my university journey the same year, choosing to study Computer Science — a field I’ve always been passionate about. I'm the only son in my family, and I have two older sisters who have always supported and inspired me. Growing up, I was always curious about how things work, especially in the digital world. This curiosity eventually led me to explore programming, video editing, and creative technologies. Today, I’m constantly learning and trying to improve myself through personal projects, freelance work, and hands-on experience.

Why programming?

At first, when I heard about software, I didn’t really understand what it was. But as I started learning more, I discovered that it has many different branches. That made me curious to find out which path suited me best. After some research and self-reflection, I realized that I’m most interested in becoming a software engineer. I learned that starting with frontend development would be the right first step on this journey. What drew me to this field is my deep curiosity for technology. I’ve always wanted to stay connected to the world of tech, and I feel uncomfortable when I’m away from it. That’s why I chose this path — not only because I’m passionate about it, but also because it’s one of the most respected and future-oriented careers.

What makes me enjoy?

Aside from coding, something else I truly enjoy and have become good at is video editing. When I first started, I was just reposting other people's videos on Instagram. But eventually, I decided to give it a try myself — and I realized I could actually do it well. Most of the edits I make are focused on cars. People often ask me, “How can you stay so patient while editing?” My answer is simple: I don’t see it as work. Work can be tiring, but I do this out of passion and enjoyment. That’s why I love it so much — when I’m editing, I completely lose track of time. I’ve never even tested the limits of my patience, because I’ve never felt like I had to. Based on this experience, I also consider myself a mobile videographer, as I create and edit all my videos using mobile tools and techniques.
